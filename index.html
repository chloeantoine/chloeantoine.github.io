Work in progress

  • Agricultural productivity and biodiversity effects: theory and evidence
    with Jean-Marc Bourgeon & Jos√© De Sousa
    abstract
    Agricultural specialization on the most suitable crop allows farmers to make the most of local land characteristics, but it also amplifies pest pressure, thereby requiring heavy pesticide use. We propose to examine this trade-off by building on ecological theory. We develop a model in which the resilience of a farmer's plot is endogenous to the crop and pesticide choices of her neighbor and derive the equilibrium distribution of land among crops. Using disaggregated data and novel instrumental variables, we then estimate the impact of diversification on global crop productivity. Results show that agricultural diversity significantly boosts yields of major crops like maize, rice, and cassava.
  • Globalization and extensive margins in agriculture
    abstract
    Anderson and Yotov (2022) introduced the Domestic Extensive Margin (DEM) to identify new trade policy effects using the gravity equation. They defined the DEM as the ratio of products actually produced by a country to the total number that could have been produced. While the denominator can be assumed to be common across countries for manufacturing goods, it varies for agricultural goods due to differences in local climate and soil conditions. For this reason, Anderson and Yotov (2022) did not include agriculture in their analysis. Using agro-ecological data on potential yields, I remedy to this gap. I establish key facts on the agricultural DEM for 130 countries over the past 60 years and quantify the extensive margins effects of globalization for this specific sector.
